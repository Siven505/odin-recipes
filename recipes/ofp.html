Old Fashioned Pancakes!

Description

These old-fashioned pancakes will make your mouth water with this great recipe, which I found from my grandma's recipe stash! These Pancakes are better than Cracker barrels pancakes! My Great-Memaw passed down this recipe when her hands started to shake back in 1984. My family keeps her tradition of making these every Sunday morning before the 9:00 service. Thought it would be time to share her recipe on the net with all of the new moms wanting traditions for their own families. These pancakes are a perfect mixture of maple and vanilla you barely need syrup. they are easy to stir your youngins can help in the kitchen. the soft fluff of the breakfast is a wonderful start to any day.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • 1 ¼ cups milk
  • 1 egg
  • 3 tablespoons butter, melted

Directions

  1. In a large bowl, sift together the flour, baking powder, salt and sugar. Make a well in the center and pour in the milk, egg and melted butter; mix until smooth.
  2. Heat a lightly oiled griddle or frying pan over medium-high heat. Pour or scoop the batter onto the griddle, using approximately 1/4 cup for each pancake. Brown on both sides and serve hot.
